— Part One

So what exactly is a balloon payment?

Let us start with the fundamentals. Once you understand how a balloon is structured, the path to handling it becomes clear.

Small monthly payments for 10 years. One larger payoff at year 10.

Your DPA second lien is structured on a 30-year amortization schedule — meaning your monthly payment is calculated as if you had 30 years to pay it off. That keeps your monthly payment very manageable at approximately $185 per month.

However, the loan itself matures at year 10. At that point, whatever balance is left becomes due. This is what is called a "balloon" — because the final payoff sits at the end like a balloon at the end of a string.

The Honest Truth

Because the early payments on the second lien are mostly interest, the balance does not pay down much in 10 years. The balloon at year 10 will be approximately the same as your original loan — around $23,000.

— Part Four

Three strategies for handling the balloon.

You have multiple paths to satisfy the balloon when year 10 arrives. Here are the three most common — each one works.

i.

Refinance into a single loan

The most common path. Use your built equity to refinance your first mortgage into a new loan, and use that refinance to pay off the DPA second lien at the same time. You walk away with one clean mortgage — and likely at a better rate than you have today.

When Anytime you have built sufficient equity, ideally years 5-10
How it works New first mortgage replaces both your current first lien and the DPA second lien — DPA gets paid off in full as part of the refinance closing.
Best for Borrowers who plan to stay in the home long term and want to consolidate into one payment
Important note The DPA program does not allow resubordination — meaning the second lien must be paid off when you refinance the first.
ii.

Sell the home before year 10

The average homeowner moves or refinances every 7 years. If life takes you in a new direction before year 10 — Nevada appreciation has built you significant equity. The sale proceeds pay off both loans completely and leave money in your pocket.

When Anytime before year 10 — common for life changes like job moves, growing families, or relocation
How it works At the closing of your sale, both loans are paid off from the sale proceeds. Whatever is left after both loans are satisfied is yours to keep.
Sample math Sell at year 7 for $605,000 (5% appreciation). Pay off ~$435,000 first lien + ~$22,000 second lien = ~$148,000 in net equity to you.
Best for Borrowers whose plans may evolve over time
iii.

Pay it off directly at year 10

The balloon balance is approximately $23,000 — manageable in a few different ways. Pay it off with savings, take out a HELOC against your equity, or refinance just the second lien on its own.

When At year 10 if you have not refinanced or sold by then
How it works By year 10 you will likely have $200,000+ in built equity. The $23,000 balloon represents roughly 10% of that equity — easily covered by a HELOC or cash savings.
Best for Borrowers who plan to stay long-term and prefer not to refinance the entire first mortgage
Bottom line Even in the worst case, your equity vastly exceeds what you would owe on the balloon.
— Part Five

Why this works in your favor.

The structure of this loan was designed knowing how most homeowners actually live in their homes. Here is why the math is on your side.

i.

Your equity will dwarf the balloon

Even at a conservative 3% annual appreciation rate, you will have built approximately $163,000+ in equity by year 10. The balloon is around $23,000. Your equity will be roughly 7x larger than what you owe on the balloon.

ii.

You have 10 full years to plan

This is not a surprise payment that catches you off guard. You know about it from day one — and you have a full decade to choose your strategy, build equity, and prepare. Most borrowers handle it well before year 10 ever arrives.

iii.

You can prepay anytime

There is no prepayment penalty on this loan. If you come into extra money — a bonus, a tax refund, an inheritance — you can pay down the second lien faster, reducing the balloon balance, or pay it off entirely whenever you choose.

iv.

Real estate is a long-term game

Markets fluctuate year to year — but over a 10-year horizon, equity reliably builds through both appreciation and principal paydown. You have time on your side.
